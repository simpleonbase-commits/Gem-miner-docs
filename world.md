# The World

The map is a **200 × 520 tile procedurally generated cave system**, seeded fresh for each session. Terrain is sculpted with layered Perlin noise, producing organic cave systems, open caverns, and rare geological formations.

## Depth Zones

The world is divided into six depth zones. Each zone brings harder ore, scarcer light, and more dangerous enemies.

| Zone | Depth | Hardness Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| Surface | 0m | 1.0× |
| Underground | 15m | 1.4× |
| Deep Rock | 75m | 2.0× |
| Deep Caverns | 175m | 3.0× |
| The Abyss | 300m | 4.5× |
| The Void | 420m | 6.0× |

## Lighting

Darkness begins at 8 tiles below the surface and intensifies with depth. Players rely on a **torch radius of ~5 tiles**. At 150m and beyond, near pitch-black conditions make navigation treacherous. Rare deep ores emit a faint natural glow — rewarding observant miners.

## World Features

- **Stalactites & Stalagmites** — naturally formed rock formations hanging from ceilings and rising from floors
- **Ruined Pillars** — ancient stone columns 2–6 tiles tall with broken tops, remnants of collapsed structures
- **Crystal Clusters** — upward-spiking gem formations found only in deep caverns
- **Goblin Camps** — stone platforms with lava fire pits, scattered throughout the deep
- **Lava Flows** — hazardous pools and rivers that deal continuous damage on contact
